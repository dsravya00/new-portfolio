    This was one of the pieces in drawing class, I chose to draw a physical object displayed in class as my subject. I took it as a challenge and used only pencil and black charcoal to create a realistic sketch. My focus was on balancing the light and shadow areas, carefully highlighting the contrasts. The light source, coming from the right, illuminated the object, and I emphasized this effect by darkening the background with black charcoal to enhance the overall composition. Overall I enjoyed using black charcoal in other work as well.

    For this project, I created a skull illustration using black ink and pen tools, focusing on shading to highlight areas of shadow. I kept the design simple, avoiding unnecessary complexity. To enhance the composition, I applied the rule of thirds, placing the skull in the center for balance and visual interest. This piece helped me practice using contrast and composition effectively while working with traditional inking techniques.

    The graphic illustration above features a panda I created using Adobe Illustrator. As a beginner, I focused on utilizing the pen tool and shape tools to outline and shape the panda’s features, applying gradients and shading to add depth. I carefully selected a background that complements the illustration, ensuring the panda remains the focal point. This sketched piece allowed me to develop my skills in vector illustration, composition, and color theory while learning how to create clean, stylized designs.

    In this combined piece, these four images above are separate from one to another. I displayed this together as it shows how I used maya software and created objects like planes, helicopters, trees and butterflies.

    This piece is from my life drawing class, where I spent a few days working on the final sketch of the baby. I found the process enjoyable and was happy with the result. Structuring the outline and shaping the form presented some challenges, but I used charcoal and colored pencils, adding subtle touches of color to highlight the shaded areas.

    I enjoyed creating Max MSP. My project titled as “Movement of Shapes” focused on using a few geometric shapes and making them move and rotate. I took this project as a challenge and I was able to create some kind of movement in the final artwork. I used jit.gl and jit.gl.handle commands to rotate the geometric shapes, which is what I wanted to feature as the main focus of my piece. And my artwork represents a day time illustrating using Max msp.

    The displayed image showcases my first experience using Blender, a software that I later found to be quite similar to Maya software. For this “Distributed Object” project, I incorporated defining a few shapes such as hands, a circle, sphere, cone, and rectangle. I applied different textures to each object, using resources from the Poly Haven site. The textures I chose were: painted concrete in green for the sphere, painted brick for the cone, pink color for the sphere, cracked mud for the hand, and cracked dry mud for the rectangle. Each texture added a distinct visual element to the objects, bringing the scene to life.
